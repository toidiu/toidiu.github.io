As engineers we are often tasked with large ambiguous tasks. This is a template for writing
useful design documents. The goal of this design document is not to have the correct answer. The
goal is to clearly capture the problem and solutions so that the team can make an informed decision
on the next steps.

<!-- more -->

## Background
A 1 paragraph summary about the larger system and problem space.  Assume the reader knows nothing
about your specific system or team.

---
## Problem
Describe the specific problem, the stakeholders, and why the problem is important enough to need a
solution now. **This is the most important section!** If this is hard to write or things are
unclear, it's a signal that we dont understand what we are trying to solve.

---
## Goals and Assumptions
Create a list of goals and non-goals to evaluate your proposals.

### Goals
- G1: requirement 1 to solve the problem
- G2: requirement 2 to solve the problem
### Non-Goals
- N1: a goal that is intentionally ignored
- ...
### Assumptions
- A1: assumption about the system or problem
- ...

## Proposals

Describe the 1st proposal and how it will function. Move supporting material to the Appendix if
section gets too verbose.

- ...

**Summary**
What the solution is, important considerations and why it is the recommended solution.
