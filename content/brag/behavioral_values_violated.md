**Sample values:** Candor, Humor, Teamwork, Courage, Optimism, Thriftiness, Curiosity, Resilience, Transparency, Diversity, Responsibility, Urgency, Empathy, Rigor, Versatility

**Teamwork/Empathy**

In my experience, a high functioning team is made up of members that feel like they can make an impact but also have psychological safety to fail while learning. This culture was threatened as leadership decided to hire 5 new engineers (team of 7) within a few months. Onboarding is especially in an async/remote team and this is especially true for junior engineers. The entire situation was exacerbated by 3 senior-tenure engineers leaving the ream within a few months around that time.

Having been in similar situations as a junior, I saw hints of stress as new engineers were expected to start delivering but had very little mentoring and connection. I decided, without being assigned, to step-in and start mentoring my teammates (3 specifically) and ensure they were succeeding.

For example, I decided to code review PRs for a junior engineer and specifically focus on Rust/codebase best practices. I slowly left more specific comments and jump on multiple calls to discuss the implications of the various design choices. Over the next few months the engineers was independently putting up high quality PRs.

In another example, I just listened as a coworker vented about having to move to a new office location. The move was tough for her due to the lack of friends, family and the remote nature of the team. While I didn't actually sove her problems, I believe these human interactions are important because they build trust and provide psychological safety.
